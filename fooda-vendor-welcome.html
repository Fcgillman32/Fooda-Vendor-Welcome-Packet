Vendor Welcome Packet

Everything you need to know to get started with Fooda.

We're thrilled to have you as part of the Fooda vendor network! This guide contains everything you need — from program expectations to day-of-event checklists.

Fooda connects great local restaurants with workplaces, bringing employees exciting food options every day. As a Fooda vendor, you play a central role in delivering that experience.

Tap any section below or use the menu to get started. Welcome aboard!

Program Overview

Here's how the Fooda program works and what's expected of you at every event.

How It Works
  • Fooda schedules you to serve at partner locations — offices, campuses, and more
  • You'll receive event confirmations with date, time, location, and setup details
  • Customers order and pay through the Fooda platform
  • Fooda handles marketing and customer communication — you focus on the food!
Your Responsibilities
  • Arrive on time and fully set up before service begins
  • Bring all required equipment and supplies (see Supplies Checklist)
  • Maintain health, sanitation, and food safety standards at all times
  • Submit temperature logs at every event — required by the Health Department
  • Leave your station clean after every event
  • Communicate proactively if any issues arise

Temp & Food Safety

A temperature log is required by the Health Department at every Fooda event. Not submitting one is a fineable offense.

Temperature Danger Zone
40°F – 135°F  ·  Keep hot foods HOT and cold foods COLD
Temp Log Instructions
1
Before you leave
Fill in Restaurant Name, Event Location, and Event Date at the top
2
Record temperatures
Log each food item Before You Leave, When You Set Up, and 1 Hour In Service
3
Corrective action
Note any action taken if food is found in the danger zone (40–135°F)
4
Submit the log
Submit via the Fooda POS system at the end of every event

Print the blank Temp Log and bring copies to every event.

Policies & Standards
Code of Conduct
  • Treat all Fooda staff, client employees, and fellow vendors with professionalism and respect
  • Do not share client location details, building access info, or internal information
  • Social media posts featuring client locations require prior Fooda approval
Food Safety & Health
  • All food handlers must hold valid food handler certifications
  • Keep all required local health permits current — copies must be in your Fooda Binder
  • Gloves and hair coverings must be worn during food service at all times
  • Temperatures must be logged and submitted at every event
Insurance Requirements
  • General liability minimum: $1M per occurrence / $2M aggregate
  • Fooda must be listed as an additional insured on your policy
  • Expired certificates may result in event suspension
Cancellation Policy
  • Cancellations must be submitted at least 48 hours in advance to avoid fines
  • Emergency cancellations must be communicated by phone — do not rely on email alone
  • Repeated cancellations or no-shows may result in removal from the program
